Strategy notes from the treasurer

Source: Nodari Kolmakhidze (treasurer / quant), April 19 2026

BTC as collateral. The strategy uses BTC as collateral and shorts BTC perpetuals to earn funding on that collateral. Some spot trades are done as part of hedge / collateral management.

Always-on positions, dynamic sizing. Trades are open at all times; their size is rebalanced hourly. The strategic goal is to keep ~50% of capital long and ~50% short, finding coins that should outperform vs. the ones being shorted.

Funding earned on collateral, not full capital. At ~3x leverage, funding income is paid on the collateral notional, not on the full traded capital, which is the structural source of BTC accumulation.

Performance fees are withdrawn periodically. Performance fees are transferred out of this account, which produces visible drops in the balance curve that are NOT trading P&L. They are deducted from the bot's gross gains.

Why a futures-only PnL reconstruction never matches perfectly. Looking only at futures-only trades won't reproduce the full PnL path: BTC collateral mechanics, spot hedging trades, and periodic fee withdrawals all affect the BTC balance independently of futures realized PnL. The most reliable performance signal is the BTC balance itself, net of deposits / withdrawals.

Methodology

Strategy: The bot trades to accumulate more BTC, not USD. All metrics are denominated in BTC. USD price changes do not affect performance measurement.

Portfolio Value = Futures wallet balance + Unrealized PNL + Spot wallet balance, all measured in BTC.

Initial capital — balance-based methodology. Initial capital at the Analysis Start Date is the actual account balance at that moment, computed by working backward from current balance:
balance_start = balance_now − settled_PnL_during − unrealized_now − deposits_during + withdrawals_during
This matches the treasurer's accounting and excludes any pre-bot personal trading churn from earlier deposits / withdrawals on the same Binance account.

Capital deployed = Initial balance at start + deposits during period − withdrawals during period.

Robot P&L = Current BTC portfolio − Total BTC capital deployed. Shows how many BTC the bot has earned (gross of any performance fees that were withdrawn).

ROI = Robot P&L / Total capital deployed. Measures BTC growth percentage.

Realized vs. Total P&L. "Realized only" excludes the current mark-to-market value of open positions; "incl. Unrealized" includes them. Because positions are always on, Total is the more representative figure.

Equity Curve = Starting capital grown by daily BTC compound returns.

Drawdown = Decline from the BTC equity peak. Max drawdown = largest peak-to-trough BTC loss.

Sharpe Ratio = (avg daily BTC return / stddev) × √365. Above 1.0 = good risk-adjusted BTC returns.

Profit Factor = Gross BTC profits / Gross BTC losses. Above 1.0 means bot earns more BTC than it loses.

Forecast = Portfolio × (1 + monthly BTC ROI)^months. Projects future BTC accumulation based on historical BTC performance.

Data integrity & forecast standards (CFI-aligned)

Source of data. All figures are taken directly from Binance API (Spot and Futures): account balance, income history, deposits, withdrawals, transfers. No third-party or estimated data; snapshot and time series are from your linked API key.

Assumptions. Forecast uses historical average monthly P&L and standard deviation; trend uses simple linear regression on monthly P&L. Three scenarios (optimistic / average / pessimistic) follow scenario analysis practice: base case ± one standard deviation. Compound projection assumes reinvestment of returns; linear projection is shown for comparison only.

Limitations. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Forecast extrapolates a short history (7 months) and does not model market regimes, volatility shifts, or funding-rate changes. All values are in BTC — the bot's goal is BTC accumulation, not USD profit.
